HTML Vocab Terms

HTML Elements

Characters:

<!-- & -->
Comment: allows for comments to be added to the code.

A:

<a> & </a>
Marks content that acts as a hypertext link.
<abbr> & </abbr>
Marks an abbreviation or acronym.
<address> & </article>
Marks contact information for an indicidual or group.
<article> & </article>
Marks a self-contained comppsistion in the document such as a news paper stoty.
<aside> & </aside>
Marks content that is related to a main article.

B:

<b> & </b>
Indicates a span of text to which attention should be drawn (text usually appears in bold).
<base> & </base>
Specifies the document's location or use with resolving relative hypertext links.
<blockquote> & </blockquote>
Contains content that is quoted from antother source, often with citation and often indented on the page.
<body> & </body>
Contains the entire content of the document.
<br> & </br>
Represents a line break within the grouping element.

C:

<cite> & </cite>
Marks a citation to a title or author of a cretive work (text usally appears in italics).
<code> & </code>
Marks content that represents computer code (text usually appears in a monospace font).

D:

<data> & </data>
Associates a data value with the marked text with the calue attrivute providiing the value.
<dd> & </dd>
Contains the description or definition associated with a term from a description list.
<dfn> & </dfn>
Marks a defined term for which a definition is given elsewere in the document.
<div> & </div>
Contains a generic grouping of elements within the document.
<dl> & </dl>
Marks a description list containing one or more dt elements with each followed by one or more dd elements.
<dt> & </dt>
Contains a single term from a description list.

E:

<em> & </em>
Indicates content that is emphasized or stressed (text usually appears in italics).

F:

<figure> & </figure>
Contains an illustration, photo, diagram, or similar object that is corss-referenced elsewhere in the document.
<figcaption> & </figcaption>
Contains the caption associated with a figure.
<footer> & </footer>
Contains closing content that concludes an article or section.

G:

H:

<h1-h6> & </h1-h6>
Marks major headings with h1 representing the heading with the highest rank, h2 representing next highest-ranked heading, and so forth.
<head> & </head>
Contains a collection of metadata elements that describe the document or provide instructions to the browser.
<header> & </header>
Contains opening content that introduces an article or section.
<hr> & </hr>
Marks a thematic break such as a scene change or a transition to a new topic(often displayed as a horizontal rule).

I:

<i> & </i>
Indicates a span of text that expresses an alternate voice or mood (text usually appears in italics).

J:

K:

<kbd> & </kbd>
Marks text that represents use input, typically from a computer keyboard or a voice command.

L:

<li> & </li>
Contains a single item from an ordered or unordered list.
<link> & </link>
Specifies an external resource that the document is connected to.

M:

<main> & </main>
Marks the main content of the document or application; only one main element should be used in the document.
<marks> & </marks>
Contains a row of text that is marked or highlighted for reference purposes.
<meta> & </meta>
Provides a generic list of metadata values such as search keywords, viewport, properties, and the file's character encoding.

N:

<nav> & </nav>
Marks a list of hypertext or navigation links.

O:

<ol> & </ol>
Contains an ordered list of items.

P:

<p> & </p>
Contains the text of a paragraph.
<pre> & </pre>
Contains a block of preformatted text in which line breaks and extra spaces in the code are retained (often displayed in a monospace font).

Q:

<q> & </q>
Marks content that is quoted from another source.

R:

S:

<s> & </s>
Marks content that is no loger accurate or relevant (text is usually struck through).
<samp> & </samp>
Marks text that represemts the sample output from a compute program or application.
<script> & </script>
Provides programming code for programs to be run within the document.
<section> & </section>
Marks content that shares a common theme or purpose on the page.
<small> & </small>
Marks side comments (text usually in small print)
<span> & </span>
Contains a generic run of text within the document.
<strong> & </strong>
Indicates content of strong importance or seriousness (text usually appears in bold).
<style> & </style>
Defines the display styles used to render the document content.
<sub> & </sub>
Marks text that should be treated as a text subscript.
<sup> & </sup>
Marks text that should be treated as a text superscript.

T:

<time> & </time>
Marks a time calue or text string.
<title> & </title>
Stores the document's title or name, usually displayed in the browser title bar or on a browser tab.

U:

<u> & </u>
Indicates text that appears stylistically different from normal text (text usually appears underlined)
<ul> & </ul>
Contains an unordered list of items.

V:

<var> & </var>
Marks text that is treated as a variable in a mathematical epression or computer program.

W:

<wbr> & </wbr>
Represents where a line break should occur, if needed, for a long text string.
